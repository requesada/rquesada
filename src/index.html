Pomodoro Blacklist

(2024)

For my foray into browser extensions, our attention-deficient society inspired me to make a Pomodoro Technique timer combined with a site blocker. The concept was to make something that felt like an analog device resting on a table, the buttons evoking tactility. I chose a color palette and used Figma to do a mockup of the front of the device, making notes about the functionality I intended to include.

To track rounds—each includes a pomodoro, or work interval, and a break—I imagined an array of LED lights that spanned the top of the device and flashed or remained lit depending on whether an interval was in progress or complete. This motif would later become the extension's icon.

I broke from tactile reality to create the options button, which flips over the device with a satisfying button push as opposed to, for instance, a click of the right side of the device. [rewrite]

The back of the device is simple, showing only inputs for interval lengths.
